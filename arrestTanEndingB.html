Sine The Sleuth

With a heavy heart, I approach mother in her arm chair. If arresting her is the only way to protect the others, I suppose it must be done. Yet if it is right, why must it feel so wrong?

I approach her with a tortured expression.

“Tangent of Tangent Enterprises, mother, you are arrested for the murder of Cotangent.”

She turns around and solemnly stares into my tear-filled eyes. She looks as though she has a list of refutations and defenses on the tip of her tongue, but I know she would never utter them to spare her son the guilt.

“Do what you must” she replies.

She stands and extends her wrists. I remove handcuffs from my belt (something I have carried with me for years in case a mystery such as this would arise) and fasten them around her wrists.

We make a call and minutes later, the PPD (Pythagorean Police Department) arrives to carry her away. She hangs her head low in the back seat of the police car as she slowly drives away from Tan Manor.

Today I learned that happiness is not infinite like a Sine graph, extending endlessly across the x axis. It is instead a Sine inverse graph: it is finite; it has a restricted domain; it lasts only for a period and never more.

Today I am no longer Sine. I am henceforth Sine^-1.

The killer chuckles under their breath as the car shrinks into the horizon. An innocent function has been arrested. Everything is going according to plan...
